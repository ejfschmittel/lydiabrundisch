Rainy and sunny days in German towns

Today, I wondered about which German towns have lovely weather. I explored some data on rainy and sunny days for all German towns. The results are very interesting: First, you might want to move to Baabe or Juliusruh by the Baltic Sea, and second, there seems to be no correlation between the two! But number of rainy days is so weirdly distributed across towns that I doubt the validity of the data. See the full R Markdown protocol. Haldane's principle and the relationship between social welfare spending and population

Today, I read the essay "On Being the Right Size" by Haldane in which some relationships between an animal's size and its anatomical and physiological features are considered. In the last paragraphs, Haldane suggests corresponding relationships between a state's size and its organizational structure, stating that socialism works optimally in small states. Vaguely inspired by this, I set out to analyze the relationship between European states' size and social welfare spending as percentage of their GDP. My operationalization of this hypothesis is highly questionable. If you trust the results (which you shouldn't), they represent weak evidence against Haldane's principle as he applied it tentatively to the field of politics. See the full R Markdown protocol. I am going to post updates as I continue to work on this project.
